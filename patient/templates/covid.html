What you should know...

What is coronavirus?

COVID-19 is a new strain of coronavirus detected in Wuhan, China, in December 2019. The novel coronavirus (nCoV) has not been previously identified in humans and can cause a multitude of illnesses ranging from flu-like symptoms to severe acute respiratory syndrome. The rapid spread of the virus has led to the outbreak being declared as a global pandemic.

What are its symptoms?

Moderate to high grade of fever, dry cough and shortness of breath are common symptoms of coronavirus infection. People with existing health conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, respiratory conditions, compromised immunity and pregnant women are at higher risk to develop a severe infection.

How does it spread?

Coronavirus infection can spread via small droplets of bodily fluids such as saliva or mucus from the nose or mouth which are released when the infected person exhales, sneezes or coughs. The virus can be transmitted when you touch objects or surfaces that have these droplets, as well as shake hands of infected persons and consequently, touch parts of your face or eyes.

How is it treated?

Currently, there are no vaccines or antiviral medications that can cure the coronavirus infection. However, certain treatments can help alleviate the symptoms of fever, cough, and breathing difficulty. People with mild infections can recover at home on semi-voluntary quarantine. However, when symptoms are not manageable at home, hospital isolation is considered.

What precautions to take to avoid the coronavirus infection...


Wash your hands frequently

Do not touch your eyes, nose and mouth

Clean your hands with sanitizer if soap is not available

Avoid going to crowded places

Cover your face when cough and sneeze into your elbow

Avoid non-essential travel to international locations

Wear a mask if you are sick or if you are a healthcare professional

Always keep medical essentials like OTC drugs, thermometer & electrolytes
